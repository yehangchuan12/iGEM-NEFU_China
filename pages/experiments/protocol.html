2. Agarose gel electrophoresis

1. Prepare an agarose gel and heat in a microwave for 2-4 minutes until the agarose is dissolved; add 1/10000 concentration of Goldview mix thoroughly.
2. Pour the gel onto a taped plate with casting combs in place. Allow 20-30 minutes for solidification.
3. Carefully remove the gel casting combs and place the gel in a horizontal electrophoresis apparatus. Add 1X TAE electrophoresis buffer to the reservoirs until the buffer covers the agarose gel.
4. Mix the agarose gel loading dye and DNA sample and load them into the wells. Electrophorese the gel at 150-200 mA until the required separation has been achieved, usually 0.5-1 hour.
5. Visualize the electrophoresis results under UV light.

3. PCR product purification

1. Add CP Buffer to the PCR products in the 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube.
2. Insert a HiBind® DNA minutes Column in a 2 mL Collection Tube.
3. Add no more than 700 μL solution from Step 1 to the column.
4. Centrifuge at 15,000 x g for one minute at room temperature. Discard the filtrate and reuse the collection tube.
5. Add 700μL DNA Wash Buffer.
6. Centrifuge at 15,000 x g for one minute at room temperature. Discard the filtrate and reuse the collection tube.
7. Repeat step 5 and step 6.
8. Centrifuge the empty column for two minutes at maximum speed (15000 x g) to dry the column matrix. Discard the filtrate and reuse the collection tube.
9. Transfer the column to a clean 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube.
10. Dry at 55-60℃ for 5 minutes.
11. Add 30μL double distilled water or Elution Buffer and centrifuge at maximum speed (15000 x g) for 1 minute.

4.Extraction of DNA from agarose gel

1.Take the column and put it on ice to activate it.
2.When the target band was sufficiently separated by agarose gel electrophoresis, the DNA fragment of interest was carefully excised using a clean, sharp scalpel while removing excess agarose. Put the gel slice in the 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube and add the XP2 Binding Buffer. Incubate at 50-60°C for seven minutes or until the gel completely melts. Vortex or shake the tube every 2-3 minutes.
3.Insert a HiBind® DNA minutes Column in a 2 mL Collection Tube.
4.Add no more than 700μL DNA/agarose solution from Step 2 to the column.
5.Centrifuge at 10,000 x g for one minute at room temperature. Discard the filtrate and reuse the collection tube.
6.Add 300 µL XP2 Binding Buffer.
7.Centrifuge at maximum speed (≥13,000 x g) for one minute at room temperature. Discard the filtrate and reuse the collection tube.
8.Add 700μL SPW Buffer.
9.Centrifuge at maximum speed for one minute at room temperature. Discard the filtrate and reuse the collection tube.
10.Centrifuge the empty column for two minutes at maximum speed to dry the column matrix. Discard the filtrate and reuse the collection tube.
11.Transfer the column to a clean 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube.
12.Dry at 55-60℃ for 5 minutes.
13.Add 30μL double distilled water or Elution Buffer and centrifuge at maximum speed (15000 x g) for 1 minute.

7.Electroporation

This protocol is based on the MicroPulserTM Electroporation Apparatus Operating Instructions and Applications Guide.
1.Wash the 0.1 or 0.2 cm electroporation cuvette (store in 75% ethanol) with tap water once and ddH2O two times and dry in the oven.
2.Place the electroporation cuvette on the ice and use UV to disinfect for 20 minutes.
3.Mix 25μL electrocompetent cells with 1 to 2μL of DNA (total DNA concentration less than ten ng/mL, maximum 25 ng). Mix well and incubate on ice for 1 minute.
4.Set the MicroPulser to "Ec1" when using the 0.1 cm cuvettes. Set it to "Ec2" or "Ec3" using the 0.2 cm cuvettes.
5.Transfer the mixture of cells and DNA to a cold electroporation cuvette and tap the suspension to the bottom. Place the cuvette in the chamber slide. Push the slide into the chamber until the cuvette is seated between the contacts in the base of the chamber. Pulse once.
6.Remove the cuvette from the chamber and immediately add 1 mL of SOC medium to the cuvette. Quickly but gently resuspend the cells with an eppendoff.
7.Please transfer the cells to the medium as soon as possible.
8.Transfer the cell suspension to a 1.5 mL polypropylene microfuge tube and incubate at 37℃ for 1 hour, shaking at 200 rpm, 37℃. 9.Check and record the pulse parameters. The time constant should be close to 5 milliseconds.
10.Plate on selective LB or SOB medium and inverted culture for 14~16 hours.

10.Plasmid Extraction

1.Isolate a single colony from a freshly streaked selective plate and inoculate a 5 mL LB medium culture containing the appropriate selective antibiotic. Incubate for ~12-16 hours at 37°C with vigorous shaking (~ 300 rpm).
2.Centrifuge at 10,000 x g for one minute at room temperature.
3.Decant or aspirate and discard the culture media.
4.Add 250 µL Solution I/RNase A. Vortex or pipet up and down to mix thoroughly. Complete resuspension of cell pellets is vital for obtaining good yields.
5.Transfer suspension into a new 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube.
6.Add 250 µL Solution II. Invert and gently rotate the tube several times to obtain a clear lysate. A 2-3minute incubation may be necessary.
7.Add 350 µL Solution III. Immediately invert several times until a flocculent white precipitate forms.
8.Centrifuge at maximum speed (≥13,000 x g) for ten minutes. A compact white pellet will form. Promptly proceed to the next step.
9.Insert a HiBind® DNA minutes Column into a 2 mL Collection Tube.
10.Transfer the cleared supernatant from Step 8 by CAREFULLY aspirating it into the HiBind® DNA minutes Column. Be careful not to disturb the pellet and that no cellular debris is transferred to the HiBind® DNA minutes Column.
11.Centrifuge at maximum speed for one minute.
12.Discard the filtrate and reuse the collection tube.
13.Add 500 µL HBC Buffer.
14.Centrifuge at maximum speed for one minute.
15.Discard the filtrate and reuse the collection tube.
16.Add 700 µL DNA Wash Buffer.
17.Centrifuge at maximum speed for one minute.
18.Discard the filtrate and reuse the collection tube.
19.Repeat Steps 16-18 for a second DNA Wash Buffer wash step.
20.Centrifuge the empty HiBind® DNA minutes Column for 2 minutes at maximum speed to dry the column matrix.
21.Transfer the HiBind® DNA minutes Column to a clean 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube.
22.Add 30μL double distilled water or Elution Buffer directly to the center of the column membrane.
23.Centrifuge at maximum speed for one minute.
24.Perform second elution.
25.Store DNA at -20°C

12.SDS-PAGE

1.Gel preparation:
Two electrophoretic glass plates are used to make vertical grooves (no glue leakage) placed vertically. Pour the prepared separation gel solution into the non-ionic water; after the gel gathers, pour out the non-ionic water, dry it with absorbent paper, pour in the concentrated glue, and then insert the comb.
2.Sample preparation:
Add 1.8 mL ddw to the colorimetric plate, take 200μl bacteria liquid, and determine OD600. Divide six by the measured OD value; the milliliters of bacteria should be taken for crushing. The bacterial liquid was taken into the EP tube and centrifuged at 4200 rpm for 5 minutes. Discard the liquid, centrifuge for a short time, and absorb the liquid with a white gun. 600μl ddw was used for heavy suspension of bacteria. The EP tube is floated in a beaker containing a mixture of ice and water, and the ultrasonic wave is broken. The project No. 1, No. 2 is changed into a 4-frame rod (when changing, the rear knob of the instrument is adjusted at the same time), the bottom of the rod is 0.5cm from the bottom of the EP tube, the power is 20%, the ultrasonic opening time is 7s, the closing time is 5s, and the working time is 5minutes (if the effect is not good, the 5minutes can be broken again). 10000rpm centrifugation 10minutes. Take the supernatant of 50μL into another EP tube, discard the supernatant (or save) T for a short period of centrifugation, and absorb the liquid with a white gun. 600μL ddw was used to resuscitate. Take the 50μL precipitate into another EP tube. Each sample was added with 10μL 6X Protein loading (containing B-hydrophobic ethanol) and preserved at four℃.
3. Remove the gel plate and immerse it in Coomassie brilliant blue dye after dyeing and electrophoresis. Microwave oven until boiling, plate shaker speed 30, shake 20-25 minutes.
4. Clean the gel with distilled water and add the decolorizing solution. Microwave oven until close to boiling, plate shaker speed 30, shake 20 minutes. Replace the decolorizing solution and repeat this step until the decolorization is complete.

Fermentation

1.Small system fermentation
1.Induction

1.The bacteria in the cryogenic storage tube were inoculated into 5mL LB for activation 8 to 12h in advance and used to configure the ZYM-5052 medium.
2.Configure ZYM-5052:

Place ZYM-5052 at 37℃ at 150 to 200 rpm for about one hour and grow to an OD600 of 0.4 to 0.8.
3. Remove the bacteria from the shaker and cool the shaker to 18℃. Add 20% arabinose (final concentration 0.2%) and IPTG (final concentration 1mM).
4. Induction at 150~200 rpm at 18℃ for about 50 hours.

2. Whole-cell catalysis

1.The bacteria were removed and placed on ice at the end of induction.
2.Determines the OD600 of each sample of bacteria and records.
3.Take several 10mL centrifuge tubes, put the corresponding OD into the tube, and centrifuge at 4200 rpm for 10 minutes as far as possible.
4.Discard the supernatant and put it on ice.
5.Preparation of fermentation broth: Use substrate mother liquor and 50mM Tris-HCl to make fermentation broth in a particular proportion.
6.Add 1mL fermentation broth to each 10mL centrifuge tube to resuspend the gentle bacteria.
7.Put the centrifuge tube into 30℃for fermentation at 200rpm for 12～18h or sample at regular intervals.
