Contribution to the alleviation of myopia that is a worldwide disorder

Based on previous reports, 7-methylxanthine (7-MX) is a potential drug to alleviate myopia,1 but the high production cost hinders its development and clinical application.2 In our expert visit, an expert of myopia treatment mentioned that if the price of 7-MX was too high, the society would not be willing to further explore its effects and make it commercial. In our project, we have developed a biosynthesis system that can efficiently convert caffeine to 7-MX with coffee residues as starting materials. Based on our experimental data, we are firmly convinced that our discovery may indeed contribute to the development of 7-MX as an anti-myopia therapeutics.

Caffeine metabolic platform (CaMePl)

Overall, our team has achieved a considerable success in the biosynthesis of 7-MX. However, previous studies have also indicated that many other methylxanthine compounds possess high medicinal values. For example, 1-methylxanthine can enhance the radiation sensitivity of tumor cells, and 3-methylxanthine has been reported as an adenosine antagonist.3 Moreover, 1,7-domethylxanthine can increase diastolic blood pressure, stimulate thermogenesis, and increase plasma adrenaline, among others. They are generally synthesized by chemical methods that need tedious steps and high cost.2 Therefore, it is urgently needed to develop cheap, efficient and sustainable synthetic systems for these valuable compounds.

In our NDM enzyme system, demethylation of caffeine can be specifically processed by the combinations of different enzymes, i.e., NdmA/NdmD for the N-1 position, NdmB/NdmD for the N-3, and the NdmC/D/E complex for the N-7. Therefore, we will further use our concept to build other biosynthesis platforms based on the activities of these enzymes. These platforms can be easily designed by the appropriate combinations of these enzymes, according to their specific activities and the features of the target compounds. Importantly, coffee residues can still be the economical raw materials to extract caffeine, which can be used as the substrate to synthesize a variety of methylxanthine compounds. We hope that this biological platform can be efficiently used to reduce the production cost of methylxanthine compounds and make important contributions to the development of the global pharmaceutical industry.
