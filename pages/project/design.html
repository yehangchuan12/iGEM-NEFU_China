Overview

A modular platform was designed and developed to optimize the synthesis of 7-methylxanthine (7-MX), a compound known for its potential to alleviate myopia, utilizing caffeine as the substrate.

Nowadays, the significant cost of 7-MX is mainly ascribed to the following two reasons. One primary reason pertains to the difficulty associated with chemical synthesis, including the low yield, high expense and considerable by-product pollutants. The second reason is that de novo synthesis of 7-MX in yeast is difficult and inefficient. The current caffeine degradation technique exhibits a maximum yield of only 0.37g/L.1 We optimized this platform through a rational design by retrieving caffeine from coffee residues. This optimization reduces the costs and enables the effective decaffeination of coffee residues. With substantially reduced caffeine content, the generated grounds can be reused for other purposes or safely disposed. All above is realized through the following two modules:

In Module 1, we constructed a high-copy plasmid expressing caffeine degradation genes. This plasmid was subsequently introduced into the BW25113 strain of Escherichia coli (E. coli) in order to facilitate the demethylation of caffeine and subsequently produce 7-MX.

In Module 2, a low-copy plasmid containing the genes for formaldehyde dehydrogenation was constructed and also transformed into the BW25113 strain. In this case, we successfully eliminated formaldehyde that is generated as a by-product during the demethylation of caffeine to produce 7-MX. Formaldehyde is not only toxic to our engineered bacterial hosts, but also harmful to the environment. Besides, this system can also provide NADH as the reducing power to the demethylation reaction.

This section outlines the process of designing the two modules, verifying their functions, and optimizing the strategy.

Module 1: the high-copy plasmid highly expressing the Ndm enzymes to convert caffeine to 7-MX

7-MX: the first reported methylxanthine compound that alleviates myopia

Methylxanthine compounds mainly come from natural plants, such as Guarana-related products2, coffee, tea and various herbal medicines3. They contain a variety of methylxanthine and polyphenols, in which the most common methylxanthines are caffeine (1,3,7-trimethylxanthine), theobromine (3,7-dimethylxanthine) and theophylline (1,3-domethylxanthine). In addition, many kinds of methylxanthines contain only one methyl group. In the early study of deprivation myopia in guinea pigs, 7-MX could eliminate eyeball elongation caused by morphological deprivation4. Another report also demonstrated 7-MX’s beneficial effects on deprivation myopia in rabbits 5 (Fig. 1), which suggested that 7-MX could be potentially used as a drug in the treatment of myopia. Recent clinical trials in Denmark further proved that 7-MX was an ideal remedy for myopia alleviation (Fig. 2).6

Fig. 1. Previously reported inhibitory effects of 7-MX on deprivation myopia of rabbits.4

Fig. 2. Previously reported inhibitory effects of 7-MX on progressive myopia. According to the baseline age of children and the average daily dose of 7-MX, a linear relationship between 7-MX dose and therapeutic effects was observed. The best fitting model was used to predict the tracks of children's refractive error (A) and axis-oculi length (B).6

Gene source

Caffeine degradation genes mainly come from Pseudomonas. Multiple caffeine degradation pathways exist, but a unique one is present in Pseudomonas CBB5, which demethylates caffeine (Fig. 3).7 We chose to use the genes in the CBB5 genome and planned to express NdmA and NdmD in engineered E. coli BW25113. Through this pathway, caffeine can be converted to theobromine, which is then converted to 7-MX by NdmB and NdmD (Fig. 4).

Fig. 3.The main reactions in the caffeine degradation pathway of CBB5 -- demethylation of caffeine at N-1 position to produce theobromine, demethylation of theobromine at N-3 to generate 7-MX, and demethylation of 7-MX at N-7 to create xanthine

Fig. 4.Construction of a metabolic pathway in engineered E. coli. NdmA and NdmD demethylate caffeine at N-1, and NdmB and NdmD demethylate theobromine at N-3 to produce 7-MX.

Production and Construction Design of 7-MX

We were originally inspired by the sequence of the Ndm genes in the CBB5 genome (Fig. 5).8

Fig. 5.Pseudomonas putida CBB5 contains the ndmA, ndmB and ndmD gene cluster.8

We constructed a plasmid containing the ABD circuit with an order of ndmA, ndmB, ndmD driven by the common promoter pBAD (Fig. 6-1), but the 7-MX production by the ABD strain was very low. After our rational analysis, we found that the activities of both NdmA and NdmB require the participation of NdmD, and thus deduced that the order of importance of the genes in 7-MX production could likely be ndmD>ndmB>ndmA (please read the Engineering page for more details). Therefore, we constructed a plasmid with the DBA circuit, in which ndmD is located downstream of a strong promoter, pBAD, and ndmB and ndmA are driven by the promoter p107 with medium strength (Fig. 6-2).

Fig. 6-1.The ABD circuit originally constructed according to the order of the CBB5 gene cluster.

Fig. 6-2.The DBA circuit operated by dual-boot subsystem showing significantly improved 7-MX production versus the plasmid in Fig. 6-1.

The high molecular weight of NdmD could be the reason for the low expression of ndmD as an exogenous protein. We hoped to improve the yield and solubility of the ndmD protein by optimizing its sequence. We noted that ndmD contains a Rieske [2Fe-2S] cluster at the N-terminal, which allows NdmC and NdmE to form a tight NdmCDE complex; however, this part of the sequence is unnecessary in promoting NdmA and NdmB activities.9 In order to reduce metabolic pressure and increase the expression of ndmD, we removed the first 266 amino acid residues of NdmD to form a truncated protein, ndmDt (for more details, please visit the Parts page). The NdmDt protein size is only 54.7% of the full-length NdmD. We put a strong promoter (pBAD) upstream of the ndmDt, and a moderately strong promoter (p107) upstream of the ndmB and ndmA to construct the DtBA circuit (Fig. 7). After comparing these three circuits with different utilizations of promoters, we ultimately selected arabinose to induce ndmDt expression and IPTG to induce ndmBA expression. The highly increased yield of 7-MX was further realized through this optimization (Fig. 8)

Fig. 7.Gene arrangement in the DtBA circuit.

How to detect it?

We detected methylxanthine by HPLC, using a C8 column and a mixture solution made of water, methanol and glacial acetic acid in a ration of 85:15:0.5(v/v) as the mobile phase.

Module 2: Construction of the cofactor regeneration system

We had learned that in the process of caffeine demethylation to produce 7-MX, NADH is used and formaldehyde is released, the latter of which is highly harmful to the human body and may pollute the environment.10 In addition, formaldehyde also has severe toxicity in the engineered E. coli cells,11 which may lead to early cell death and reduce the transformation efficiency. Therefore, we decided to design a cofactor regeneration system based on formaldehyde dehydrogenase to eliminate formaldehyde produced in the demethylation process and provide NADH for the reaction.

frmAB knockout in the E. coli genome

In the E. coli genome, the formaldehyde dehydrogenase frmA and frmB can convert formaldehyde to formic acid.12 Therefore, we first wanted to verify whether the expression of frmAB in the original genome could be sufficient to dehydrogenate the formaldehyde produced by caffeine demethylation. Using the homologous recombination method, a common approach used in our laboratory, we knocked out the frmAB genes in the E. coli genome to get the BW2-Δfrm strain. Subsequently, we compared the production levels of the wild type and BW2-Δfrm strains, which could indirectly assess the adequacy of endogenously expressed frmAB in eliminating formaldehyde in Module 1 (Fig. 9).

Fig. 9.Evaluation of caffeine demethylation by the endogenous frmAB in E. coli. Using the homologous recombination method to replace, the frmAB in the genome was replaced by the resistance gene kana. Meanwhile. the frmAB gene was transferred into the DtBA plasmid shown in Fig. 6-3. The effect of the endogenous frmAB is indirectly verified by comparing the yield of the two strains.

Verification of formaldehyde dehydrogenase and formate dehydrogenase

We transformed the low-copy plasmids with frmAB, FDH, or both into the BW25113- Δ frm strain together with DtBA (Fig. 10). We hypothesized that formate dehydrogenase FDH alone had minimal effect on the reaction, and it was necessary to cooperate with FrmAB to augment the effect. Eventually, we found that the strains with the genomic frmAB, exogenously introduced frmAB and FDH could achieve the maximum 7-MX production efficiency.

Fig. 10.Three low-copy plasmids containing frmAB, FDH or both, and the DtBA plasmids were double-transformed into BW-Δfrm to verify their effects.

How to test?

We have learned that formaldehyde causes toxicity to E. coli cells and leads to a decrease in production yield. Thus, we used an indirect approach to test the effect of each enzyme on the reaction through examining the 7-MX yields of these strains under different conditions by HPLC, as shown in Fig. 9.

Two-module interaction system

We hoped to utilize Module 1 for the degradation of caffeine to produce 7-MX, while Module 2 would be used to eliminate the formaldehyde formed in Module 1. Additionally, Module 2 could produce NADH, which would be fed back into Module 1 to enhance the forward reaction. This approach aimed to establish a sustainable and efficient production process with minimal adverse impact on the environment. (Fig. 11).
