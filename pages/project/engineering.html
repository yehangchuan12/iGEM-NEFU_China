Overview

Our team achieved efficient and low-cost production of 7-methylxanthine (7-MX) through building a caffeine metabolism platform in E. coli. Meanwhile, by constructing a cofactor cycle, we released the reducing power in the 7-MX manufacturing system, which fits our goal of environment-friendly production.

Cycle 1: construction of a gene pathway according to their arrangement in the CBB5 genome

design

In Pseudomonas putida CBB5, NdmA and NdmB are independent Rieske nonheme iron monooxygenases with N-1 and N-3 specific N-demethylation activities, respectively. Their activity depends on the oxidation of NADH by NdmD to provide electrons and catalyze the N-demethylation reaction1. Therefore, we decided to construct a gene pathway following their gene locus arrangement in the CBB5 genome. Next, we transformed the created plasmid into E. coli to test the function of NdmA, B and D, aiming to achieve the goal of converting caffeine to 7-MX.

Build

We synthesized the ndmA, B and D genes, and obtained their coding sequences by PCR. Then, we ligated them downstream of the pBAD promoter in the expression vector using the Golden Gate assembly method. After the successful construction, the plasmid was transformed into E. coli BW25113, and the strain ABD was obtained.

Learn

The production of the trace amounts of 7-MX and theobromine by the ABD clone conceptually proved that the ndmA, B and D genes were all expressed and functional in our constructed plasmids. The extremely low yield of the products could be due to the insufficient expression of NdmD. Inadequate supply of NdmD could have led to suppressing NdmA and NdmB activities. In the meantime, the results showed that the intermediate product theobromine has obvious accumulation relative to 7-MX. We predicted that the expression of NdmB was insufficient, which hindered the conversion of theobromine to 7-MX. Therefore, we intended to improve the expression of these key genes by altering the arrangement sequence of the ndmA, B and D genes in the vector.


Cycle 2:adjustment of the gene arrangement


Design

Among the NdmA, B and D, NdmD is a highly specific reductase for the Ndm enzymes and provides the major power for the reaction1. Both NdmA and NdmB are virtually dependent on NdmD for their proper functions.

In the caffeine breakdown pathway, the enzyme NdmA initiates the process by catalyzing the removal of the N1-methyl group from caffeine, resulting in the formation of theobromine. Next, the enzyme NdmB utilizes theobromine as a substrate and facilitates the removal of the N3-methyl group, leading to the production of 7-MX. To enhance the expression of the crucial ndmD gene and improve the catalytic efficiency of NdmB in converting theobromine to 7-MX, we modified the gene arrangement to the order of DBA.

Build

The NdmD coding sequence was placed in the first place, driven by the strong pBAD promoter. After adjusting the sequential order of the ndmA and ndmB coding sequences, the Lac operon and the medium-strong promoter p107 were used to activate the expression of the ndmB and ndmA genes, which are all assembled into a vector using the Golden Gate method. The generated plasmid was transformed into E. coli BW25113 to obtain the strain DBA.

Figure 3.Gene arrangement of the DBA circuit.

Test

After the induction of DBA, the yield of 7-MX was increased by 28.3 folds, and no obvious theobromine accumulation was detected.

Learn

The SDS-PAGE analysis (Fig. 4A) revealed a significant proportion of the NdmD protein in inclusion bodies, indicating its quick expression in the host bacteria and its poor solubility led to the formation of inclusion bodies and reduced the effectiveness of the reaction.


Cycle 3:truncation of key genes

Design

After analyzing previous literature, we found that both the N-terminal Rieske type 2Fe-2S domain and the interdomain sequences are dispensable for electron transfer of NADH. The removal of this sequence from the N-terminal region of NdmD will unlikely impact its enzymatic activity and thermal stability2. Therefore, we planned to truncate this key gene by removing the redundant N-terminal and interdomain sequence, which may reduce the metabolic pressure of E. coli and subsequently increase the solubility of NdmD, leading to further increased 7-MX production.

Build

Using the ndmD coding sequence as the template in PCR amplification, we obtained the ndmDt fragment, which lacked the coding region of the excess 266 amino acids at the N-terminus. The PCR fragment was ligated together with the ndmB and ndmA coding sequences into a vector using the Golden Gate assembly. The generated plasmid was transformed into E. coli BW25113 to obtain the strain DtBA.

Figure 5. Gene arrangement of the DtBA circuit.

Test

The expression of the DtBA clone was induced and verified by SDS-PAGE. Although NdmDt still partially formed inclusion bodies, its expression in the soluble form considerably increased and the 7-MX yield significantly elevated. Compared to the results of the previous two cycles, the 7-MX yield increased from 0.7% to 71.1%, which was over 100-fold escalation.

Cycle 4:setup of the cofactor cycle


Design

NdmA and NdmB release methyl groups from the N-1 and N-3 sites of caffeine molecules, respectively, which are usually further oxidized to formaldehyde by oxygen3. The generated formaldehyde can be highly toxic to both the engineered bacteria and the environment. Thus, we decided to set up a cofactor regeneration system to express formaldehyde dehydrogenase (frmAB) and formate dehydrogenase (FDH) induced using the arabinose-induced promoter. This endeavor aimed to accomplish the objective of efficiently degrading formaldehyde into carbon dioxide, thereby minimizing environmental pollution and simultaneously releasing reducing power in the reaction system.

Figure 8.Schematic diagram for the overall interaction between the DtBA enzymes and cofactor regeneration system.

Figure 9.The simplified overall circuit of cofactor regeneration system.

Build

The frmA, frmB and FDH fragments obtained by restriction endonuclease digestion were ligated into a low-copy vector by the Golden Gate assembly. The constructed DtBA and pSB1c-frmAB-FDH plasmids were double-transformed into E. coli BW25113.

Test

The induction of the cofactor regeneration system and DtBA circuit allowed us to increase the yield of 7-MX to 8mM (1.33g/L), which was 260.36% higher than the DtBA circuit alone. The data indicated that our cofactor system could greatly improve 7-MX production by the DtBA circuit.

Figure 10.Comparison of the 7-MX productions by the DtBA and DtBA+frmAB-FDH clones determined by the whole-cell catalysis under the condition of 25(C, 10 OD600 and 8mM caffeine for 18 h.

Learn

Through the implementation of the cofactor regeneration system, the dual benefits of eliminating the potential pollutant formaldehyde and supplying NADH for the reaction are achieved. This therefore enhances the capacity of engineered E. coli to break down caffeine. The practical significance of removing formaldehyde generated during the reaction has been demonstrated.


Conclusion

In conclusion, through rational design of the metabolic circuits and optimization of the reaction process, we have successfully attained the goal of cost-effective and resource-efficient production of 7-MX through environmentally sustainable manufacturing practices.
